Identity and Values

I would like to consider myself an explorer. The German word "Fernweh" describes my desire to discover a bigger world and experience different things.

Growing up in a small city Nanning and being close to my whƒÅnau, I have developed an enormous love for my hometown and my family. In our culture, we value being around our parents and extended family. But there is also a saying: It is better to travel ten thousand miles than to read ten thousand books. My grandma once told me to study abroad and explore the world when I grow up. She planted the seed in my mind, and now it has grown like a strong tree. I will always stay curious and hungry on my journey to learn new things, embrace different cultures, and discover the world.

The three most important values to me are kindness, respect, and empathy.

I like to feel how people feel and to understand everyone might be going through something you know nothing about. The open-mindedness and belief in equality have long been embedded in my mind, which require me to treat everyone as equal regardless of their age, gender, race, beliefs, etc.

When it comes to the ethical decision I made, I left my previous job due to a coworker's racist speech. The coworker consistently complained about another employee to me in our language with offensive words. It had a huge conflict with my value as what she did was not respectful or nice. If I were the other employee, I definitely do not like to be judged just because of my skin and have offensive words that I couldn't understand against me in my ears. And I found out the coworker wasn't the only one. It made me question the environment of the work as it is where I don't enjoy working even I only worked part-time. I have a nature of running away from things I do not like. So I left the job and started looking for a workplace where is more ethical and inclusive.
